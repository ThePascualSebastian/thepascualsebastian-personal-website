My Story

My path into computer science started in high school, during a summer internship at T-Mobile. I watched engineers debug backend systems, coordinate in Jira, and ship fixes that affected real inventory data — and I was hooked. That was the moment I realized I wanted to build things that actually matter.

From there I enrolled at Clemson University and threw myself into CS — balancing coursework, projects, and internships. At the Naval Information Warfare Center, I built an adaptive learning platform that cut military onboarding time from three months to one month. That project taught me what it means to write software with real stakes — real users, real impact.

Now I'm a Data Engineer Intern at InvestiNet, building the data infrastructure that keeps business operations running. My first project was standardizing the database connection layer across the entire codebase — designing a unified, reusable framework with retry logic, rollback handling, and automated notifications. It's exactly the kind of foundational, high-impact work I love.

I'm bilingual in English and Spanish, which has shaped how I communicate and collaborate across teams. Whether it's a code review or a whiteboard session, I care about clarity just as much as correctness.
